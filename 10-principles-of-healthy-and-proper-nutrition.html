Proper nutrition: 10 golden rules

Nutrition is a very valuable health tool. To be able to define “correct”, it must be based on a diet (to be understood as a set of foods you eat regularly and not as a “diet”) that meets the following characteristics: healthy, well-balanced, varied, which provides for the introduction of all the food groups in the right proportions.

Here are the golden rules:

1. Consume fruits, vegetables and vegetables in abundance: multiple portions throughout the day. In addition to being low-calorie and high-volume foods (therefore also with high satiating power), they are particularly rich in minerals and vitamins, valuable substances for the body and that protect against diseases such as tumors and pathologies affecting the heart and arteries.

2. The daily diet should never lack bread, pasta, rice and cereals, basic nutrients of the diet, since they constitute the energy source of ready consumption.

3. Alternate in the diet meat, fish, eggs that contain animal protein, essential for the formation of muscles.

4. Regularly take milk, yogurt and cheeses that are characterized by the intake of calcium, proteins of high biological quality and some vitamins. Even better if partially skimmed.

5. Limit the consumption of seasonings, fats and sweets. With regard to seasonings, it is always preferable to consume vegetable oils, in particular extra virgin olive oil.

6. Limit salt consumption.

7. Drink at least 1.5 liters of water during the day. Limit the consumption of industrial sugary/energy drinks.

8. Always start the day with a hearty breakfast in the morning.

9. Avoid continuous out-of-meal snacks (especially sweet / savoury industrial snacks). A good habit are instead a mid-morning snack and a mid-afternoon snack with fruit (fresh or dried) or yogurt or even a ham sandwich!

10. Do not skip meals.
